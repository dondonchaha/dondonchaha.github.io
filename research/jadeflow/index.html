Research of Jade Flow

To embark on the design, Jade Flow cooperated with National Taiwan University Institute of Fisheries Science, Dr. Hong-Nong Chou Lab. Through a series of laboratory tests to find the appropriate one, Haematcoccus Pluvialis(HP in short) showed the most remarkable response toward light.

HP is a freshwater species of Chlorophyta from the family Haematococcaceae. This species requires a pH neutral environment and be kept free of contamination by unwanted algae, fungi and protozoa. With the beating of the cilia, HP shows positive phototaxis, which results in the flow in the brighter area of the water. Slightly yet unceasingly it moves.

Thus, abundant experiments were devised regarding HP’s habits to generate the most outstanding effect. The influence of different factors on the algae, such as lighting type and intensity, vessel shape and size, as well as gravity and temperature were measured.

The experiments showed that the most conspicuous phototaxic effect appeared when HP was housed in a standing thin plate with the light placed beneath. Based on the outcome, we adapted the disc-shaped and begun designing the embossment of the vessel to channel the phototaxic movement.
